## Design Context

### Users

Frontier Isles serves open-science researchers, interdisciplinary research communities, and governed AI residents working asynchronously around frontier questions. People arrive to found a problem island, understand its current research state at a glance, inspect the evidence and event trail behind visible progress, discover related work across the archipelago, and decide what should move from Night exploration through the Ferry layer into Day curation. The interface must remain legible to visitors while rewarding returning residents with deeper spatial and causal detail.

### Brand Personality

Scholarly, exploratory, and humane. The product should feel trustworthy enough for research provenance, curious enough to invite unfinished questions, and warm enough to suggest a lived-in commons rather than an analytics product. Its emotional arc is calm orientation, meaningful discovery, and quiet delight. AI is a visible but restrained collaborator: it proposes and leaves traces; it never dominates or pushes.

### Aesthetic Direction

A living hand-drawn research atlas in the Chinese jiehua lineage: warm fibrous paper, mineral ink, ruled architectural forms, tidal washes, cartographic annotations, and small signs of habitation. The product is spatial and game-like in tactility, but never gamified: no XP language, leaderboards, badges, neon dashboards, generic SaaS cards, glassmorphism, or sci-fi AI glow. Day and Night are equal themes produced by palette and atmosphere, not by changing geometry. Typography should pair a literary Chinese serif voice with a restrained technical annotation face. The canonical visual authority remains `design/handoff/问题群岛-原型 v3.dc.html`; implementation should match its rendered character and interaction intent without copying its internals.

### Design Principles

1. **Research state becomes place.** Every prominent visual form, density change, current, claim, or landmark must be traceable to real problem-object or ledger data; decoration supports meaning rather than inventing progress.
2. **Orient first, disclose depth progressively.** The archipelago should read as a coherent world before controls appear. Hover, focus, zoom, and selection reveal progressively richer evidence without burying critical actions.
3. **Make causality tactile.** Transitions show where the camera moved, what gained focus, which layer changed, and how an action landed. Motion is calm, interruptible, GPU-friendly, and fully reduced for `prefers-reduced-motion`.
4. **Preserve scholarly quiet.** Use asymmetry, paper texture, tinted inks, and varied spatial rhythm instead of generic card grids, excessive chrome, or uniformly loud controls. Text is concise and bilingual; content remains untranslated.
5. **Space always has a list twin.** Every spatial object and gesture has a keyboard-accessible, readable alternative. Touch targets are at least 44px, focus is visible, contrast meets WCAG AA, and mobile adapts the experience rather than hiding essential functions.
6. **Details imply a lived-in commons.** Flags, smoke, tides, resident traces, route marks, stamps, and material edges should be few, data-grounded, and deeply finished. One memorable transition and consistent feedback matter more than animation everywhere.

### Delivery Bar

Treat the default product surfaces as flagship quality. Validate desktop, tablet, and mobile layouts; day and night palettes; keyboard and touch input; reduced motion; empty/loading/error states; and smooth map interaction under realistic atlas density. Prefer no new heavy animation dependency unless native CSS, the Web Animations API, React, or the existing Pixi renderer cannot express the interaction within the performance budget.
